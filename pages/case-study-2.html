The start

This project started a little different. I had the opportunity to be there while the stakeholders were brainstorming about the next steps for their business. They suggested to do a web site to get a bigger audience and set the stage to create a online shop after a year. They sent me a brief, and we had a discussion, from which we concluded to first do a kind of portfolio for the shop.

The process

I started with researching the business. The company is a workshop for handmade designs mainly made of copper, brass and silver wire. They work with handmade jewelry, as well as products for interior design (lamps, vases, clocks etc.) Through this platform they will expand their customer base, as well as form partnerships. The customer’s range is pretty big, mainly women age 30-50, for the jewelry, and/or their husbands searching for a gift. The company firstly needs a good web platform to showcase their products, and gain a bigger customer range. Since they have unique products and also do custom work, the contact page is very important.

The Product

The product is stylish and minimalistic with a twist. The menu is a pop up that moves in on the whole page where the users can choose the category they want to explore. The categories are the story of the company, where the customers can get aquatinted with the deep history of the family business. Then they can see the different types of products and the motive behind them. Afterwards I showcased a gallery of collections, and ended with a contact form. Besides the menu, the customers can scroll through the categories on the pages. This solution gives us a good start to build a customer base and gain brand awareness.

The Lesson

After presenting the finished product, having worked in a team for the first time on a real project, I learned a lot about myself, as well as about design principles. The biggest lesson would be that the saying – “Less is more” does not always apply. Meaning, we were so focused on making the cards simple, that we cut out a lot of information, which could make one candidate possibly stand out.
